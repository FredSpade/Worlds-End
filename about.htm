In a far-flung corner there is a place....
World's End is a very mixed-up place.
Surreal, silly, melancholy... Not much is taken too seriously. Fun with genre cliche, mixed up cultural references. Nerdish stuff. NPCs break the fourth wall, complaining about their futile, scripted existence.
Some NPCs are certain the game world is real, while others are incomprehensible and many are simply violent.
Why?

Explore 2D biomes, discover secrets in 256 colours!
There's a fortune out there for sure....

Like most things here, character classes are a bit mixed up and a little bit real.

It takes time to traverse the world... and effort... maybe some thought and a little skill.
There are a multitude of creatures and inhabitants to encounter.

There are nasty traps, a riddle or puzzle or two and some tricky combat situations.

But nobody understands why all living things harness the elemental energy they call Magic.

It joins gunpowder, the blade, tooth and claw in a world gone mad.

Punks and Thugs roam the cities, looking for trouble.
Scammers want your money. Robbers wait on the roads....

At the beginning and the end of time, simultaneously.
A shattered reflection in extreme beauty and brutality.
Nobody can understand our place in the universe.
Not even if they cared.

Why is this place so messed up?

It's messed up because it takes place generations after an apocalyptic event.
Some other, possibly, unrelated situation eventually leads to the player investigating theories into the origins of the world. They must go to the furthest or most obscure places seeking answers which result in revelations about the world's geology, its species and catastrophic history.
There would be a point where the truth is revealed, something like the Statue of Liberty in Planet Of The Apes. Another previous society was responsible for creating this mess with a technology undreamt of. Now civilisation is dead for the children of a hard reset.

When it finally happened, the fallout wasn't about just physics.... This was an absolute and fundamental re-arrangement of physical and conceptual reality, as if proceedurally re-generated from random numbers. Trees and houses continued to exist. This wasn't the destructive power of the ancient atom bombs. Even past and present seemed to merge. Fantasy became reality and vice versa. Surreality shifted ground as continents reshaped and all the colours of the rainbow changed their places.
Civilisation ended in that moment. Time froze but life clung on. The landscape was completely different, yet somehow familiar, but from the primordial chaos of the crunching random numbers also came confusion, savagery and violence where knowledge and wisdom might have been.
